## Executive Intent

PropArt should monetize as a serious creative business platform, not a collection of hobby tools.  
The monetization model should emphasize business outcomes, unified plan logic across the suite, and polished product/brand behavior at every touchpoint.

**Core thesis:**  
Sell speed-to-market and business readiness ("from one photo to clean product pack in minutes"), then package that value in a single subscription family with clear limits and premium expansion paths.

---

## Positioning Principle: Monetize Without Looking Cheap

### 1) Sell outcomes, not features
- Preferred message: **"Turn one photo into a clean product pack in minutes."**
- Avoid feature-led framing like "AI crop + export + notes" as the primary headline.
- Product copy should answer:
  - What job gets done?
  - How much time is saved?
  - What business result is unlocked?

### 2) One flagship subscription family
- Keep tools separate by workflow (Hub, Cropper, Studio, Picker, etc.).
- Keep pricing unified by one plan family across suite surfaces:
  - **Creator (Free)**
  - **Pro (Most Popular)**
  - **Studio (Business)**

### 3) Useful free tier, but value gates at business boundaries
- Free should prove value fast and build trust.
- Business-value gates should remain paid:
  - batch processing
  - cloud project continuity
  - watermark removal / branded exports off
  - team collaboration and controls

### 4) Annual anchor pricing to signal SaaS maturity
- Monthly plan for flexibility.
- Annual plan with clear savings (target 15-25%).
- Keep price architecture simple and clean:
  - Example anchors for testing: **$19 / $49 / $99**

### 5) One premium add-on, not many micro-charges
- Select one add-on that deepens account value:
  - team seats
  - Suite Vault
  - advanced workflow automation
- Avoid fragmented upsells that make the platform feel low-trust or gimmicky.

## Plan Architecture (Proposed)

### Creator (Free)
- Manual crops
- Limited exports
- Watermark on outputs
- Local-only projects

### Pro (Most Popular)
- Full auto-crop
- Unlimited exports
- No watermark
- Cloud save
- Project continuity across suite tools

### Studio (Business)
- Multi-seat access
- Shared Vault
- Role controls
- Priority support
- Bulk workflows
- Invoicing-ready billing features

**Packaging notes**
- Highlight Pro as "Most Popular" consistently in all pricing presentations.
- Keep tier language stable across all apps and docs.
- Validate price points by conversion and retention, not preference surveys alone.

## "Not Cheap" Design and Trust Signals

### Cross-suite design consistency
- Single design system across all app surfaces:
  - type scale
  - spacing rhythm
  - radii
  - elevation/shadows
  - icon style
  - motion/tone

### Product language consistency
- Use the same nouns across all tools:
  - **Projects**
  - **Vault**
  - **Exports**
  - **Automation**

### Enterprise-trust surfaces
- Publish and visibly link:
  - privacy policy
  - terms
  - security posture
  - uptime expectations
  - support SLA for paid tiers
  - billing FAQ

### Upsell hygiene
- One upgrade CTA per major surface.
- No repetitive popups or aggressive interruption patterns.

### Billing UX quality bar
- Transparent limits by tier.
- Proration clarity for upgrades/downgrades.
- Plan compare page with plain language.
- Respectful cancel/reactivate flows.

## Risks and Mitigations

### Risk: Feature sprawl causes pricing confusion
- Mitigation: strict three-tier structure + one premium add-on only.

### Risk: Aggressive gating weakens free-tier activation
- Mitigation: preserve meaningful free outcomes; gate business-scale features only.

### Risk: Inconsistent copy/visual language across apps
- Mitigation: central design system + naming governance + reusable pricing components.
